Places to go

Rijksmuseum

One of the world's most famous museums it is placed here, in Amsterdam. Rijksmuseum has a vast collection that reflects more than 800 years of history. You can see famous Dutch artists such as Rembrandt and Van Gogh, and their most popular works of art.

Van Gogh Museum

Right next to the Rijksmuseum you can find Van Gogh Museum. Van Gogh was a Dutch artist known by his dramatic brushstrokes and his peculiar temperament. In Van Gogh Museum you can see some famous works of arts, such as "The Potato Eater" and "Sunflowers".

Anne Frank House

The journal written by Anne Frank when she was only 13 years-old became one of the books most readen in the world. You can know more about her life during the Second Great War in Anne Franks Museum. The building, located in central Amsterdam, is the same where Anne hid with her family from the nazis.

Bloemenmarkt

The Netherlands is well known for its amazing tulips fields. If you love flowers, when you come to Amsterdam, don't let it check the Bloemenmarkt. The world's only floating flower market was founded in 1862, and It has 15 souvenir and gift shops, with only a few still selling fresh flowers.

NEMO Science Museum

NEMO Science Museum is the top attraction this vibrant if you are a family. Take a stroll along the canal by the central station and you cannot miss this boat shaped building filled with experiments that is fun for anyone between 2 and 99.

A'DAM Tower

A swing at the top of one of the very few high rise buildings of this city giving you the thrills while being able to enjoy the city view is a place to be if you are not one of those faint hearted ones. The bonus is that it comes with a bar!

Zoo

Zoo is always a place to go when visiting as a family, even more if it is one of the oldest ones in Europe. The butterfly garden and the planetarium makes it Artis more special. Dont miss picnicking at Artis when the weather is sunny, it will surely be a day well spent.

Windmills

To put a tick on visiting a windmill, nothing comes closer to Zaanse Schans as it offers more than its breathtaking view. Spices being ground, clogs being made, diamonds getting cut and top it all with some authentic Dutch pancakes.
